Key Information Relating to This Project

Corporate Bonds enable investors to invest their funds at the Shojin company level. Those funds are then used by the company for:
  • Underwriting new projects - these are new projects which may not be fully funded on the day that the underlying property purchase needs to complete. Shojin will fund them for a short term while continuing to sell the project on the platform. While the project is underwritten Shojin earns the rate of return on the project.
  • Investing into Shojin projects - Shojin may take longer term positions in various Shojin projects and earn the rate of return on the project.
  • Funding short term cashflow - Shojin earns its’ income predominantly from project completions rather than ongoing management fees. Although the profits can be very good, this makes Shojin's cashflow very lumpy. Corporate bonds enable Shojin to fill any short term funding gaps.
